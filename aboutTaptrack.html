TapTrak - A spinal tap training assistant that helps construct a mental model using real-time feedback based on needle tracking, and allows physicians to choose the kind of spine they want to work with.

Features

Needle Tracking and Fault Detection

Physician can monitor trajectory of the needle in 3D space and is alerted when a mistake is made.

Multiple Inserts

Multiple spinal blocks allow for abnormal anatomy to be accommodated.

Customizable

Cuztomize features to specific skill-levels

Procedure Feedback

An array of visualizations, statistics and video replay provides in-depth analysis of the procedure.

How It Works

The physician selects the type of spine and inserts this into the main manikin frame to begin the procedure.

As the needle propagates through the skin, the physician is able to see the trajectory of the needle on screen.

If something goes wrong, the physician will be alerted, and allowed to try again.

Needle Tracking and Fault Detection

For tracking, the needle will be painted black, with bright spots placed along its length. In the dark, the light source helps the camera identify the position of these spots, that can be used to display the needle trajectory, overlayed on the digital reconstruction of the spinal insert.

The system will alert the physician when a fault is encountered. This could include an optional audio alert.

Hardware

Two cameras and a light source surrounding the spinal insert are mounted on the current manikin. The back mesh is embedded with piezoelectric sensors which will indicate the starting position of the needle.

Camera 1: Amscope

The Amscope has a built-in light source with eight LEDs to illuminate the spinal region of the manikin. The microscope has a CMOS color sensor, and operates on USB power. The image capture and editing software provides still image and live video capture and editing capability.

Camera 2: Android Endoscope

The Android Snake Video Borescope is a highly portable industrial videoscope that connects to a smartphone. And it is built with six adjustable LED lights for illumination.

Piezoelectric sensors

To allow the detection of the needle on the surface of the skin.

Multiple Inserts

A diverse array of inserts are included to accommodate abnormal anatomies of the spine.

These inserts would have a 3D-printed bone structure embedded in a gel block. Each insert would have a corresponding digital persona that would be used to provide visual feedback to the physician.

Customizable

Different modules that provide varying levels of educational training aid can be built based on the skill-level of the physician.

Procedural Feedback

Physicians can choose to view and learn from:

Video replay of the procedure.

Statistics indicating speed, direction, number of failures.

Visualizations based on their training trends.
